# Peanuts, Rewards, and Why They Matter

Every time you learn something in My Backpack, you earn peanuts. Peanuts are My Backpack's in-app currency — earned through genuine learning and redeemed for things that have real value. This document explains how the system works, why it's designed the way it is, and what it means for learners of different ages.

---

## What Are Peanuts?

Peanuts are the currency of learning in My Backpack. They are simple, visible, and tied directly to educational progress. You earn them by doing the work — not by logging in, not by watching, but by answering questions correctly, completing lessons, and maintaining your learning streak.

The name is intentional. Peanuts are small individually, but they add up. A learner who works consistently through the week accumulates real purchasing power over time. The peanut is also inherently playful — appropriate for the young children who are My Backpack's most enthusiastic earners.

---

## How You Earn Peanuts

Peanuts are awarded for meaningful learning activity:

- **Completing a roadmap node** — finishing a lesson and reaching the passing score earns a peanut reward set by the teacher or content creator who designed the node
- **Mastering a term** — when a vocabulary term's confidence score reaches the mastery threshold (0.85), you earn peanuts
- **Daily learning streaks** — returning to learn on consecutive days earns bonus peanuts
- **Quiz performance** — earning more points per question (by answering quickly and correctly) earns more peanuts than grinding through with multiple attempts

Peanuts are never awarded for simply opening the app, watching content passively, or spending time without engaging.

---

## What Peanuts Buy

### For Children
Children redeem peanuts for tangible rewards that parents have pre-approved:

- Toys and games (age-appropriate, from a curated catalogue)
- Stickers and collectibles
- Experiences (a trip to the movies, a specific outing chosen by the family)
- In-app items (avatar accessories, profile decorations)

Redemption happens at a My Backpack testing centre, where a parent accompanies the child to collect a physical reward. Alternatively, an online redemption option will be available for parents who prefer home delivery.

Parents can see their child's peanut balance at any time from the owner profile. The parent controls what categories of reward the child can save towards.

### For Adults
Adults redeem peanuts for:

- **Real money** — withdrawn at a testing centre, with identity verification
- **Vouchers** — for retail, food, or services from partner businesses
- **Charitable donation** — donate your peanuts to an educational cause of your choice

The adult redemption model means that genuine learning has a direct financial payoff. An adult who works through a full subject syllabus earns meaningful peanuts over time — not life-changing money, but a tangible return on the time invested in learning.

## XP and Peanuts — What's the Difference?

My Backpack tracks two separate reward measures:

**XP (Experience Points)** is the detailed, internal measure of learning activity. It tracks every answer, every point scored, every question attempted. XP is granular and precise — it feeds directly into the adaptive learning engine that calculates your confidence scores and learning velocity.

**Peanuts** are the simplified, visible currency that learners actually see and spend. They are derived from XP — think of peanuts as the user-friendly version of what XP is measuring underneath.

This separation exists because the internal tracking system needs to be precise (for the algorithm to work), while the reward system that motivates learners needs to be simple and emotionally compelling (for behaviour change to happen). You don't show a 5-year-old a confidence score of 0.73. You show them a peanut count of 47 and a toy they want at 100.

## Why Tangible Rewards Work

Research in educational psychology consistently shows that intrinsic motivation (learning because it's interesting) is the most durable driver of long-term engagement. But intrinsic motivation takes time to develop — and for many learners, particularly young children and adults returning to learning after a break, it is not yet in place.

Tangible, immediate rewards bridge the gap. A child who earns a sticker today keeps coming back tomorrow. An adult who earns real money for genuine learning is far more likely to maintain a consistent routine than one who earns a digital badge.

My Backpack's rewards are designed to:

1. **Be immediate** — you see peanuts added right after the correct answer
2. **Be concrete** — peanuts have a clear value, not vague "progress"
3. **Require real learning** — you can't earn them without genuine engagement
4. **Connect to something desired** — children choose what they're saving for, creating a personal goal

---

## The Testing Centre Connection

Peanuts connect the digital and physical worlds. The most meaningful redemptions — both the best toy rewards for children and the cash withdrawals for adults — happen at My Backpack testing centres. This is intentional.

A testing centre visit is a significant event. It's where a learner writes a formal assessment. It's also where they collect their accumulated rewards. The combination turns a test day into a celebration of genuine progress — not a source of anxiety.

🔜 The peanuts wallet, XP service, and reward redemption system are designed but not yet built. They are a Phase 3 feature. The models and data structures that support them are already in place.
