# How My Backpack Makes Money

My Backpack is being built to serve South African learners first. Monetisation is a second-phase priority — the core product must be solid and genuinely useful before any revenue model is introduced. This document outlines the current thinking on how My Backpack will eventually sustain itself.

🔜 **None of the monetisation models described below are built yet.** They represent planned approaches for Phase 3 and beyond. The current version of the product is free to use.

---

## Guiding Principle

Any revenue model must be consistent with the mission. My Backpack exists to make quality education accessible to South African learners. A monetisation model that prices out the learners who need the platform most would undermine everything the product stands for.

The working model is **freemium** with meaningful free access, supplemented by institution-level subscriptions, testing centre fees, and other revenue streams that don't extract value from individual learners.

---

## Revenue Models Under Consideration

### 1. Freemium — Free Base, Premium Features

**How it works:** Basic access to My Backpack is free for all learners. A premium tier unlocks additional features.

**What stays free:**
- Core learning experience (roadmaps, quiz sessions, vocabulary)
- Adaptive learning engine
- All existing question types
- Profile system (up to 6 profiles)
- Basic progress tracking

**What premium unlocks (potential examples):**
- Unlimited roadmap access (free tier may have a monthly limit on new content unlocked)
- Advanced analytics for learners (detailed confidence tracking, velocity data)
- Priority question generation
- More profiles per account
- Ad-free experience (if ads are introduced on the free tier)

The specific free vs premium split is to be determined once the product has sufficient users to understand which features people value enough to pay for.

---

### 2. Teacher and School Subscriptions

**How it works:** Schools and teachers pay a monthly or annual subscription for access to the teacher dashboard, class management tools, and content creation features.

**Why schools would pay:**
- Teacher dashboard with class-level progress monitoring
- Content creation tools (roadmap builder, question builder)
- PDF and textbook upload pipeline (AI generates study material)
- Class assignment and management
- Learner performance reports aligned to CAPS requirements

**Pricing model (to be defined):**
- Per teacher (individual teacher subscription)
- Per school (site licence based on learner count)
- District or government level (bulk licensing)

This is likely to be one of the stronger revenue streams because schools have procurement budgets and the value proposition (reducing teacher workload while improving learner outcomes) is measurable.

---

### 3. Testing Centre Fees

**How it works:** When a learner is ready to write a formal assessment, they book a slot at a My Backpack testing centre. A fee is charged per assessment attempt.

**The value proposition:**
- Learners pay for the assessment, not the learning
- Fees are affordable relative to the cost of failing and resitting a formal exam
- Testing centres provide invigilation, official results, and peanut redemption
- Learners choose when to pay — only when they believe they're ready

**Revenue structure:**
- Fee per assessment session (paid by learner or parent)
- Possible employer or bursary sponsorship (sponsor a learner's testing costs)
- Government subsidy potential (if testing centres become part of the formal qualification pathway)

---

### 4. Content Marketplace

**How it works:** Teachers create high-quality content (roadmaps, question sets, study material) that other schools and learners can use. Teachers earn a revenue share when their content is purchased or used.

**Why this works:**
- Great teachers are monetarily undervalued in South Africa — this creates a new income stream
- Quality content created by real teachers is better than AI-generated content
- Network effects: more teachers creating content means more value for all learners

**Revenue structure:**
- My Backpack takes a platform fee (e.g. 30%)
- Teachers receive the remainder directly
- Pricing could be per-access (learner pays to unlock a specific roadmap) or per-school (school pays for a licence to a teacher's content library)

---

### 5. Peanuts Top-Up (Parent Gifting)

**How it works:** Parents can purchase additional peanuts as a gift for their child — for birthdays, academic achievements, or just because.

**The value proposition:**
- Peanuts have real-world value (redeemable for toys, money)
- A "peanut gift card" is a meaningful, education-linked present
- No gambling mechanics — peanuts are earned by learning; purchased peanuts supplement earned ones, they don't replace them

**Note:** This model must be designed carefully to avoid creating a pay-to-win dynamic where wealthy children can accumulate more rewards than learners from lower-income households.

---

### 6. Corporate and Enterprise Sponsorship

**How it works:** Companies sponsor learning for their employees or for learners in communities they want to support.

**Examples:**
- A company sponsors peanut rewards for learners in specific schools (CSI / education impact)
- An employer pays for employee access to professional development content on My Backpack
- A foundation sponsors free premium access for learners from low-income households

**Why companies would do this:**
- Education CSI is a well-established budget line for South African corporates
- Measurable impact (actual learning outcomes) is more compelling than traditional donations
- Employee learning and development is a genuine HR need

---

## What Won't Be Done

**No selling of learner data.** Learner performance data, progress, and behaviour are never sold to third parties. This is a foundational principle.

**No paywalling core learning.** The adaptive engine, the quiz system, and basic roadmap access will always be free. Revenue is generated from premium features, institution subscriptions, and the testing centre pathway — not from making learning inaccessible.

**No dark patterns.** No countdown timers designed to create artificial urgency. No features locked mid-session. No mechanics designed to frustrate free users into upgrading.
