# My Backpack — The Pitch

---

## The Problem

South Africa has 18 million school-going learners. The majority of them attend under-resourced schools with overcrowded classrooms, undertrained teachers, and textbooks that don't arrive until March. In 2023, fewer than 40% of Grade 12 learners achieved university entrance. Millions more never make it to Grade 12 at all.

The problem is not that South Africans can't learn. The problem is that the system is built around averages — average class size, average pace, average exam date — and the average doesn't exist. Every learner is different. Every home is different. And in a country with 11 official languages, teaching one learner and teaching another can require entirely different approaches.

Existing digital tools don't fix this. Duolingo teaches Zulu as a foreign language for Americans. Khan Academy is brilliant but American-focused, passive, and not built for our curriculum. Google Classroom is a file-sharing system, not a learning engine.

No one has built the tool that a South African learner actually needs.

---

## The Solution

**My Backpack** is a digital education platform built from the ground up for South African learners — from a 4-year-old learning IsiZulu vowel sounds to a 17-year-old racing through Grade 12 Mathematics ahead of their class to a 27-year-old building their vocabulary outside of any formal system.

One app. One account. Up to 6 profiles — parents and children, adults and teenagers, all learning at their own pace on the same platform.

My Backpack is not a content library. It is a personalised learning engine. It watches how each learner learns, adapts to their pace, and tells them — with precision — when they are genuinely ready to sit a formal exam. Not when November arrives. When they're ready.

The addressable market is not a niche. It is a nation.

## How It Works — Three Steps

**1. Learn at your pace.**  
Choose your subject. Work through structured roadmaps — lesson by lesson, at whatever speed suits you. The app adapts to how fast you're learning and adjusts what comes next.

**2. Earn real rewards.**  
Every correct answer earns peanuts — My Backpack's currency. Children redeem peanuts for toys. Adults redeem for real money. Learning has a tangible payoff.

**3. Test when you're ready.**  
When the app says you're ready, you are. Book a slot at a My Backpack testing centre and write a formal, invigilated assessment. No waiting for November. No writing exams you're not prepared for.

## What Makes My Backpack Different

### vs Duolingo
Duolingo teaches Zulu as a foreign language. My Backpack teaches IsiZulu to native speakers, aligned to the CAPS curriculum. Duolingo has no formal exam pathway. Duolingo's rewards are digital badges. My Backpack's rewards are real.

### vs Khan Academy
Khan Academy is exceptional at delivering video content. It does not adapt to individual learners — it provides the same content to everyone. It has no South African curriculum alignment, no IsiZulu, no rewards, and no path to a formal qualification.

### vs Google Classroom / Microsoft Teams
These are tools for teachers to share files and set homework. They are not learning engines. They do not adapt, they do not track mastery, and they do not motivate learners.

### vs Siyavula
Siyavula is South African, CAPS-aligned, and respected. It is a practice tool for existing learners, not an adaptive learning engine. It does not serve young children, does not have IsiZulu as a first-class language, has no rewards system, and has no testing centre pathway.

**My Backpack's unique position:**
The only platform combining adaptive pace + CAPS curriculum + IsiZulu as a first-class language + tangible rewards including real money + a pathway to formal qualifications — designed for multi-generational South African households.
