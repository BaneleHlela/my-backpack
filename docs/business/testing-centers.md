# Testing Centres — The Physical Layer

My Backpack is a digital product, but it has a physical ambition: to create locations where South African learners can write formal assessments when the app says they're ready. Testing centres are what make My Backpack a complete alternative to the traditional exam calendar — not just a learning tool, but a pathway to real qualifications.

🔜 **Testing centres are a Phase 4 feature.** The app must reach sufficient scale, content breadth, and credibility before a physical network is viable. This document describes the concept and long-term vision.

---

## The Concept

In traditional education, exams happen on fixed dates regardless of learner readiness. November is exam month for Grade 12. Your readiness is irrelevant — the calendar decides when you write.

My Backpack proposes a different model. The learner studies at their own pace. The app tracks their mastery across every topic in a subject. When their mastery across the full subject reaches a threshold — when they are genuinely, measurably ready — the app sends an alert.

The learner then books a slot at the nearest My Backpack testing centre, shows up on a date of their choosing, and writes a formal, invigilated assessment.

The result is official. The learner moves on.

---

## Why It Matters

**For the learner who is ready early:** A Grade 11 learner who has mastered the Mathematics syllabus in August should not wait until November. They should write in August and spend September learning something else.

**For the learner who needs more time:** A learner who is not ready in November should not be forced to write. They should continue studying and write when they're ready — without the stigma of a recorded failure.

**For the adult learner:** An adult who never finished matric should not have to return to a full-time school programme to earn a qualification. They should be able to study at their own pace, in their own time, and write when ready.

Testing centres make this possible.

---

## How It Works — Step by Step

**Step 1: The learner studies.**  
Working through My Backpack roadmaps, the learner builds mastery topic by topic. The adaptive learning engine tracks confidence per topic across every node in the subject.

**Step 2: The app monitors readiness.**  
Readiness is calculated as an aggregate score across all topics in the subject. The algorithm weights topics by their importance to the subject and penalises topics where mastery is decaying (not being reviewed).

**Step 3: The app alerts the learner.**  
When the readiness threshold is reached, the learner receives an in-app notification: *You're ready to sit the [Subject] assessment.*

**Step 4: The learner books a slot.**  
Directly from the app, the learner browses available testing centres and open slots. They select a date and time, pay the assessment fee (if applicable), and receive a confirmation.

**Step 5: The learner writes.**  
At the testing centre, the learner writes the assessment on a computer or tablet provided by the centre. An invigilator is present. The assessment is time-limited and standardised.

**Step 6: Results are official.**  
Results are issued by My Backpack (or, in future, by an accredited assessment body). The result is documented and, eventually, recognised formally.

**Step 7: Peanuts redeemed.**  
The testing centre visit is also where accumulated peanuts are redeemed — toys for children, money for adults, vouchers for everyone. The physical visit becomes a celebration of learning, not just an exam day.

## What Testing Centres Provide

Each testing centre is a managed space that offers:

**Computing infrastructure:**
- Computers or tablets for writing assessments
- Stable internet connection
- My Backpack app access for the assessment

**Invigilation:**
- A trained invigilator present throughout
- Identity verification at entry
- No mobile phones or unauthorised resources during the assessment

**Official results:**
- Digital certificate issued after the assessment
- Result stored permanently in the learner's My Backpack profile
- Physical result letter available on request

**Peanut redemption:**
- Curated toy catalogue for children
- Cash withdrawal facility for adults (with identity verification)
- Voucher options for all age groups

## Locations

Testing centres will initially be established in areas of highest demand and highest learner density. Priority areas include:

- Major South African cities (Johannesburg, Durban, Cape Town, Pretoria)
- High-density township areas where learners have the greatest need
- Areas near existing testing infrastructure (driving licence centres, SAPS offices, libraries)

The first phase of testing centres may be operated by My Backpack directly. As the network grows, a franchise or partnership model could allow community organisations, schools, and entrepreneurs to operate certified testing centres under the My Backpack brand.

---

## The Accreditation Vision

The long-term ambition for My Backpack testing centres is formal accreditation — recognition by South African qualification bodies that results from these centres carry the same weight as results from traditional institutions.

The most relevant body is **UMALUSI** — the Council for Quality Assurance in General and Further Education and Training. UMALUSI is the body that accredits assessments for the National Senior Certificate (NSC) and other qualifications.

Partnering with UMALUSI (or a similar accreditation body) would mean that a learner who writes a Mathematics assessment at a My Backpack testing centre earns a result that is indistinguishable — legally and academically — from a result earned through a CAPS-aligned school.

This is a long-term vision. The path to accreditation requires demonstrated quality, scale, security, and sustained learner outcomes. My Backpack will pursue this as the product matures and the evidence base builds.
